CINEMATOGRAPHY

What is Cinematography

Cinematography is the art of capturing motion pictures to tell a story. From how the camera is angled to lighting that sets the scenery , cinematography plays a crucial role in the entertainment we see a lot. Though we may not focus on it sometimes, it is always there! And if we look closer with a new perspective, you can see the details from the cinematographers that have the opportunity to further drive the story into a whole different world..

Innovations in Cinematography

Autonomous Drones

Capturing aerial footage involves an intricate process that involves much effort to acquire the shot you desire. However, Autonomous Drones changes that, by innovating the way drones acquire video footage without the need of a trained human operator.

Cloud Computing

What is a movie or tv show when no one is able to see it or what about if you’re having trouble with rendering certain files? That is where cloud computing gets introduced and can help out. Cloud computing provides not only power but efficiency for producers needing the resources to render more complex computer generated images (CGI) or stream a movie effectively without losing quality

VR Cameras

Imagine being able to sit back and put on a VR headset and literally be in a movie because that’s what VR cameras are capable of providing! This technology opens doors of opportunity for filmmakers looking for a challenge to depict a story in a different manner and also for a depiction of a much immersive experience.

The Future of Cinematography

Like other forms of art, Cinematography is capable of advancing throughout the years. Whether it’s a new way of getting shots or an efficient way of distributing the final film, Cinema is bound to change in this ever progressing world

But, how can we do that?

When Goodfellas was being filmed, Martin Scorsese, recalls the unexpected improvised joke from Joe Pesci in a scene in which fully depicts their dark nature and he kept it in. But why mention this?. Why is this so important in further progressing filmmaking? Martin Scorsese demonstrates us this one key idea which is vital to answering this question: Always be open to new ideas If we are not open to new ideas, how are you going to expect growth within skill sets and how your shots are produced. Martin Scorsese didn’t expect this improvised line from his actor but he overall embraced it by keeping it within the movie knowing it was going to pursue a more in-depth analysis of the characters. All in all, to truly innovate a piece of cinema, it does not have to be the craziest idea, only that in the end all that you’re pursuing is a more deeper and story driven piece of filmmaking to be produced.
